Classroom brief: To study an AI course and implement it in infographic design.
For this project, I selected the 'Elements of AI' course (https://www.elementsofai.com/) to study in detail. The task involved thoroughly understanding the course content and creating infographic posters in A3, A4, and A5 sizes. This exercise helped me practice data chunking by progressively condensing the information to fit each format, ensuring clarity while adapting from A3 to A5. The activity combined in-depth learning with the challenge of visually communicating complex ideas across different scales.

Snippets from the process

Concept

My content is designed for corporate professionals, typically aged 40 to 50, who, according to an online survey I conducted, have a limited understanding of AI and often overlook its potential to streamline their work processes. Despite the opportunities AI offers to simplify tasks, many in this demographic remain unaware of its benefits.

To address this, I’ve made AI concepts more accessible by drawing parallels between the functions of the human brain and key aspects of AI, breaking down complex topics into simpler, relatable terms without relying on technical jargon. In simpler terms, I’ve compared each chapter from the AI course to a function of the human brain, making the concepts easier to understand and more relatable.
